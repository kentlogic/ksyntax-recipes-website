Stuffed Crabs
( Rellenong Alimasag )

Ingredients:

  1. 5 lbs (2.25 kg) fresh crabs (about 10 small fresh crabs or 5 medium ones)
  2. 8 cups (2 liters) water
  3. 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  4. 2 tablespoons oil
  5. 3 cloves garlic, crushed with the side of knife and minced
  6. 1 small onion, minced
  7. 1 small tomato, diced
  8. 2 potatoes, peeled and diced
  9. 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  10. 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  11. 1 egg, beaten
  12. 1 tablespoon cornstarch dissolved in 2 tablespoons water
  13. 1 tablespoon breadcrumbs
  14. Sweet and Spicy Sauce (page 21) or banana ketchup

Cooking Procedure:

  1. Wash the crabs thoroughly under running water.
  2. Bring the water and 1 teaspoon of the salt to a boil in a saucepan.
  3. Submerge the crabs and boil for 5 to 10 minutes.
  4. Take the crabs out and set aside.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of the oil to a skillet over medium heat.
  6. Add the garlic and saute until lightly browned.
  7. Add the onion and saute until translucent.
  8. Add the tomato and potato and saute for 10 minutes, or until soft.
  9. Add the crabmeat, soy sauce, remaining 1/ 2 teaspoon of salt and the pepper.
  10. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring frequently.
  11. Remove from the heat and let the mixture cool to room temperature.
  12. Fill each crab shell with the mixture, pressing lightly with a spoon.
  13. Brush egg over the mixture. Lightly drizzle the cornstarch mixture over the filling (use the back of a spoon to spread it around).
  14. Then sprinkle and lightly press the breadcrumbs on top of the cornstarch.
  15. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of the oil to a skillet over medium heat.
  16. Place the shells stuffing-side down and fry for about 15 minutes, or until lightly browned.
  17. Alternatively, bake in a preheated oven at 425°F (220°C) for 15 minutes stuffing side up.
  18. Serve with steamed rice and banana ketchup or Sweet and Spicy Sauce

Makes 6 servings

Lusog Notes: This seafood dish is rich in vitamin B12, which helps prevent anemia.
