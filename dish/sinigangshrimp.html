Sinigang Shrimp Soup

Ingredients:

  1. 10 unripe tamarind pods, shelled (or 2 tablespoons tamarind concentrate or 1 packet sinigang powder, page 16)
  2. 12 cups (3 liters) water
  3. 5 cloves garlic, crushed with the side of knife
  4. 1/4 lb (125 g) daikon radish, chopped
  5. One 2-in (5-cm) piece ginger, peeled and sliced
  6. 1/4 lb (125 g) taro root (or potato), peeled and cubed
  7. 1/4 lb (125 g) okra, trimmed and sliced diagonally
  8. 1 tomato, sliced
  9. 1 lb (500 g) fresh shrimp, heads and shells on
  10. 1/2 lb (250 g) Chinese (Napa) cabbage or bok choy (or spinach), chopped
  11. 1 teaspoon salt
  12. 5 tablespoons fish sauce (plus extra for dipping if desired)

Cooking Procedure:

  1. Boil the shelled tamarind in a saucepan with 4 cups (1 liter) of the water for about 20 minutes until soft.
  2. The tamarind has to be submerged.
  3. Add more water if necessary.
  4. Using a fine-mesh strainer, strain the mixture into a bowl, mashing the pulp with the back of a spoon to force it through the strainer.
  5. Discard the pulp and seeds.
  6. Set aside the tamarind liquid.
  7. Pour the remaining 8 cups (2 liters) of water into a stockpot, and bring to a boil. Add the garlic, radish, ginger, taro root, and tamarind liquid (or the tamarind concentrate or the sinigang powder) and cook over medium heat for 15 minutes.
  8. Add the okra, tomato, shrimp, Chinese cabbage, and salt, and 5 tablespoons fish sauce and cook for 15 more minutes.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice and fish sauce, if using.

Makes 4-6 servings

Preparation time: 25 minutes
Cooking time: 30 minutes
