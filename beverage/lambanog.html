Lambanog Making Process

  1. Coconut tree flower are cut from the stems.
  2. Bamboo containers are attached to the stem of the coconut tree flower stems. Coconut sap called "tuba" will drip to the container.
  3. By the next morning the coconut sap is collected and placed in containers.
  4. The coconut tree flower stem is then pruned so that new sap can be collected the next day.
  5. The sap is then left for 1 day to ferment.
  6. The sap then undergoes the distillery process.
  7. After distillery, the drip is often thrown away, due to that it contains high levels of methanol.
  8. Lambanog is then bottled for sale.
